About

One page for everything affecting your day.

CityPulse aggregates community reports and public data so you can see — at a glance — whether your neighborhood is having a power outage, water advisory, internet disruption, or road closure.

Why this exists

Most utilities have a dashboard. They're slow to update, hard to find, and live on five different websites. By the time the electric company confirms an outage, your neighbors already know. CityPulse turns that informal knowledge into a real-time map.

How it works

  1. Neighbors report. Anyone can submit an incident in under 30 seconds — no account required.
  2. Reports cluster on a map. Multiple reports in the same area raise confidence that something is really happening.
  3. Public feeds enrich the data. Where available, we pull from utility status pages, 511 traffic feeds, and local emergency RSS.
  4. Alerts go out. Subscribers get an email when something matching their filters is reported.

Data sources

Currently integrated (replace with your local sources):

  • Community reports — stored in browser localStorage (MVP); migrate to a database for production
  • Local power utility outage map (public JSON endpoint)
  • Department of Transportation 511 traffic feed
  • Water utility advisory RSS
  • City emergency management Twitter / Mastodon feed

What this is not

⚠ Not an emergency service

If you see downed power lines, a fire, a gas leak, or anyone hurt — call 911. CityPulse is informational only and should never delay an emergency call.

Roadmap

  • SMS alerts (Twilio)
  • Historical incident archive & trends
  • Push notifications via Web Push API
  • Multi-city support & community moderation
  • Public API for journalists and researchers

Open source

The entire frontend is static HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Fork it, fix it, deploy your own city's tracker. Backend integrations are designed as Netlify Functions stubs ready to be filled in.
